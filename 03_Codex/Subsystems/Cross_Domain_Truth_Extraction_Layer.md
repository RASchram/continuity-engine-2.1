# Cross‑Domain Truth Extraction Layer  
*The Resonance Engine of CE 2.1*

---

## 1. Purpose of This Layer

The Cross‑Domain Truth Extraction Layer is the subsystem that gathers truths from many domains, tests them for resonance against a problem, and identifies the structural models that explain why those truths exist.

This layer is the **bridge** between:

- domain knowledge  
- structural invariants  
- model selection  
- problem‑specific architecture  

It is the subsystem that allows CE 2.1 to grow stronger over time as new domains and new truths are added.

---

## 2. Core Mechanism: The Tuning Fork

This subsystem operates on a simple but powerful principle:

**Every Domain Truth is a tuning fork.  
The problem is the resonant chamber.  
If the truth vibrates, the structure matches.**

This resonance is not emotional.  
It is structural.

A Domain Truth resonates when:

- its shape matches the shape of the problem  
- its behavior predicts the problem’s behavior  
- its constraints mirror the problem’s constraints  
- its logic stabilizes the question  

Resonance is the signal that the truth is structurally relevant.

---

## 3. The Four‑Phase Process

The Cross‑Domain Truth Extraction Layer runs through four phases:

---

### **Phase 1 — Domain Expansion**

The steward continuously expands the domain library.

Domains may include:

- physics  
- biology  
- psychology  
- engineering  
- economics  
- mythology  
- medicine  
- systems theory  
- ecology  
- leadership  
- narrative  
- mathematics  

Each domain contributes **Domain Truths** — stable patterns, laws, or behaviors.

This library grows over time.

---

### **Phase 2 — Truth Extraction**

From each domain, the steward extracts truths such as:

- “Contrast reveals hidden structure.”  
- “Systems seek equilibrium.”  
- “Stress reveals weak points.”  
- “Boundaries define behavior.”  
- “Energy follows the path of least resistance.”  
- “Feedback loops determine stability.”  

These truths are not tied to the domain.  
They are **portable structural elements**.

They form the raw material of the subsystem.

---

### **Phase 3 — Resonance Testing**

Each Domain Truth is held like a tuning fork and struck against the problem.

The steward listens for:

- **Resonance** — the truth vibrates with the problem  
- **Dissonance** — the truth contradicts the problem  
- **Silence** — the truth is irrelevant  

Resonance indicates:

- structural alignment  
- predictive power  
- explanatory potential  
- invariant emergence  

This is the moment where the steward feels the “click.”

---

### **Phase 4 — Structural Model Hunting**

Once a truth resonates, the steward asks:

**“Why is this truth true in that domain?”**

This question reveals:

- the mechanism  
- the model  
- the architecture  
- the structural engine  

The steward then “sniffs around” inside the domain to find:

- the model that produces the truth  
- the structure that forces the truth to exist  
- the mechanism that explains the behavior  

This model is then mapped onto the problem.

This is how the steward finds:

- the right architecture  
- the right analogy  
- the right structural solution  

This is how contrast theory emerged in medicine —  
not from copying medicine,  
but from discovering the **structural necessity** of contrast.

---

## 4. Outputs of This Layer

Every cycle produces:

- a set of resonant Domain Truths  
- the structural models behind those truths  
- cross‑domain invariants  
- a structural map of the problem  
- candidate architectures for solution design  

These outputs feed directly into:

- the Continuity Core  
- the Domain Rotation Protocol  
- the UMA integration layer  

This subsystem is the **engine of structural insight**.

---

## 5. Failure Modes

### **5.1 Truth Overload**
Too many truths tested at once.  
Recovery: reduce to one truth at a time.

### **5.2 False Resonance**
Emotional or narrative resonance mistaken for structural resonance.  
Recovery: ask “What mechanism makes this true?”

### **5.3 Shallow Model Hunting**
Accepting the truth without finding the structure behind it.  
Recovery: dig deeper into the domain’s internal logic.

### **5.4 Domain Narrowing**
Relying on too few domains.  
Recovery: expand the domain library.
